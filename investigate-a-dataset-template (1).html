Introduction¶

  • The percentage of females that showed-up for appointment is higher than the females who didn't.

Conclusions¶

Results :¶

  1. I checked if gender was a depending factor for no-show response received from patients,by carrying out operations to know how many females missed their appointments and how many didn't, how many males missed their appointments and how many didn't. The result of my findings showed that more females than males actually missed, and not missed their medical appointments. However, there are limitations to my research,thus this result is not sufficient to strongly say that gender is truly not a depending factor for the no-show responses.
  1. I also checked if medical issues are also a depending factor for no-show response received from patients. The result of my findings shows that there are much higher number of hypertensive/diabetic patients who didn't miss their medical appointment. Due to the limitations in my findings, there are not enough evidences for me to strongly state that these medical issues(Hypertension and Diabetes) are actually enough to predict the no-show responses observed from the patients in the dataset.

Limitations:¶

  1. I observed that some patients are neither enrolled on scholarship, and they also have all zero "0" value for the following column headers: "alcohol","diabetes","handicap", but they showed up on their appointment date. The question is,what could have brought them to the hospital in the first place? I could dive deeper into analysis with additional information, but were not gven in the dataset.
  1. I couldn't show strong correlations because most of the column headers have categorical values, e.g "alcohol","diabetes","handicap","scholarship" all having 0 or 1 values, and the "no_show" with a "Yes" or "No".
